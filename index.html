Sierpinski Triangle

How it works:

  1. This method starts with a triangle and a random point inside it.
  2. It then repeatedly:
    • Selects a random vertex of the triangle
    • Draws a point halfway between the current point and the selected vertex
    • Uses this new point as the starting point for the next iteration
  3. As this process continues, a fractal pattern emerges: the Sierpinski Triangle.

What makes this interesting is that from simple rules and randomness, a complex and beautiful geometric pattern emerges. This demonstrates how order can arise from chaos, a concept found in many natural phenomena, such as:

  • Snowflake formation
  • Plant growth patterns (e.g., ferns, romanesco broccoli)
  • River networks and drainage patterns
  • Lightning branching
  • Crystal growth
  • Galaxy formation

These phenomena, like the Sierpinski Triangle, exhibit complex structures that emerge from simple underlying rules or processes.
